# PHOTOGRAPHY GUIDELINES
## Le Club des Voyageurs

---

## 1. PHOTOGRAPHY PHILOSOPHY

### Core Principle
**Authentic & Documentary**

Photography should feel real, lived-in, and honest. Not staged influencer content. Think: photo-essay in a travel magazine, not Instagram aesthetic.

### Visual Story
We document moments of connection, curiosity, and reflection. Photos show:
- People in their element
- Genuine interactions
- Moments of discovery
- Local atmosphere & texture

---

## 2. SUBJECT MATTER

### What to Shoot

✅ **People** :
- Travelers in authentic moments
- Experts/hosts in natural settings
- Local interactions & connections
- Candid moments (not posed)
- Real expressions (curiosity, wonder, reflection)

✅ **Destinations** :
- Authentic local scenes
- Specific details (architecture, food, textures)
- Golden hour / atmospheric light
- Hidden spots, not just postcard views
- Show local life, not just landmarks

✅ **Details & Textures** :
- Markets, street scenes
- Traditional crafts
- Food & local culture
- Maps, guides, travel artifacts
- Weather & light interactions

### What to Avoid

❌ **Stock Photo Vibes** :
- Overly posed or artificial scenes
- Perfect Instagram influencer content
- Generic sunset/ocean shots
- Too-processed or heavily filtered

❌ **Poor Choices** :
- Selfies or overly casual shots
- Blurry or poorly lit images
- Too dark or overexposed
- Cluttered or distracting backgrounds
- Gimmicky angles or perspectives

---

## 3. LIGHTING & ATMOSPHERE

### Ideal Lighting

**Golden Hours** (best):
- Early morning (sunrise +1-2 hours)
- Late afternoon (sunset -2-3 hours)
- Warm, directional light
- Long shadows create dimension

**Natural Light** (good):
- Soft daylight (cloudy days)
- Window light (for indoor scenes)
- Avoid harsh midday sun
- Backlighting for atmosphere

**Mood** :
- Warm color temperature (not cold/blue)
- Atmospheric haze acceptable
- Light grain/texture welcome
- Moody/dramatic acceptable (not cheesy)

### Avoid

❌ Harsh midday sun
❌ Neon/artificial indoor lighting
❌ Overly bright/washed out
❌ Cold fluorescent light

---

## 4. COLOR & PALETTE

### Color Harmony

Photos should naturally complement brand palette:
- Warm earth tones (beige, sand, warm grays)
- Blues (sky, water, naturally occurring)
- Golden/orange accents (sunlight, fabric, food)
- Avoid neon or artificial colors

### Saturation

- **Natural colors** (not oversaturated)
- **Slight warmth** boost acceptable
- **Not overly filtered** or HDR-processed
- **Film look** acceptable (vintage quality)

### Specific Color Guidelines

**Backgrounds** :
- Neutral tones preferred
- Blurred backgrounds often better than sharp
- Avoid bright neon signs in background

**Subjects** :
- Natural skin tones (not orange from filters)
- Fabric colors true to reality
- Nature colors warm but realistic

---

## 5. COMPOSITION

### Framing

✅ **Good Practices** :
- Natural, unforced framing
- Breathing room around subjects
- Interesting depth (foreground/background)
- Asymmetrical/rule-of-thirds composition
- Multiple subjects in frame (for interaction shots)

❌ **Avoid** :
- Dead center framing (too symmetrical)
- Cropped awkwardly
- Too tight (no breathing room)
- Cluttered or busy backgrounds

### Perspective

- **Eye-level** : Most shots, creates connection
- **Slightly low** : Subjects feel more prominent
- **Detail/macro** : Texture and close-ups
- **Wide/environmental** : Context & landscape
- **Avoid** : Extreme angles that feel gimmicky

---

## 6. ASPECT RATIOS & FORMATS

### Variety is Key

Don't use the same ratio for every shot:

- **16:9** (Landscape) : Wide environmental shots, travel scenes
- **4:3** (Slightly square) : Portraits, medium compositions
- **1:1** (Square) : Details, close-ups, specific moments
- **9:16** (Portrait) : Vertical formats, creative use

### Resolution

- **Minimum** : 1920x1080px (for web)
- **Preferred** : 3000x2000px or higher (for flexibility)
- **Print** : 300 DPI at print size

## 7. EDITING & RETOUCHING

### Philosophy

**Minimal intervention**. Let the moment shine. Don't over-process.

### What's OK

✅ **Light adjustments** :
- Exposure correction
- Gentle contrast boost
- White balance adjustment
- Shadow/highlight recovery

✅ **Texture** :
- Light grain/noise (vintage feel acceptable)
- Slight vignette (edges slightly darkened)
- Film-like processing
- Subtle color cast (warm preferred)

✅ **Cleanup** :
- Remove obvious distractions
- Clone out temporary signs/ads
- Minimal skin retouching (natural)
- Remove dust/sensor spots

### What's NOT OK

❌ **Over-processing** :
- Heavy filters (Instagram vibes)
- Extreme saturation
- Blown-out sky with fake HDR
- Over-sharpened (looks fake)
- Extreme vignette

❌ **Manipulation** :
- Removing people/elements
- Changing sky/weather artificially
- Excessive skin smoothing
- Unrealistic color shifts

### Editing Software

Recommended:
- Lightroom (for batch processing)
- Capture One (professional)
- Photoshop (for retouching)
- Avoid: Heavy filters from mobile apps

## 8. QUICK CHECKLIST FOR PHOTOGRAPHERS

**Before Shoot** :
- [ ] Scout location (lighting, backgrounds)
- [ ] Check weather forecast
- [ ] Plan for golden hour timing
- [ ] Backup batteries/memory cards
- [ ] Brief on mood/vibe needed

**During Shoot** :
- [ ] Capture candid moments (not posed)
- [ ] Vary angles & distances
- [ ] Pay attention to backgrounds
- [ ] Get close-up details
- [ ] Respect subject comfort

**After Shoot** :
- [ ] Backup files immediately (2 copies)
- [ ] Light cull (remove obvious rejects)
- [ ] Organize by folder structure
- [ ] Edit for consistency
- [ ] Final quality check

---

## 9. TONE & FEELING

Every photo should feel like:

✅ **Honest** — Real moments, not staged
✅ **Warm** — Welcoming & human
✅ **Curious** — Interesting details, perspective
✅ **Authentic** — True to location & culture
✅ **Elevated** — Well-crafted, not random
✅ **Lived-in** — Story & emotion present

Not:

❌ Corporate or sterile
❌ Overly Instagram-filtered
❌ Staged or artificial
❌ Generic or touristic
❌ Cold or uninviting
❌ Too polished/fake
